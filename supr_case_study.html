Supr Daily started as a Milk Subscription and reinvented the product to become a ‘Micro-Delivery’ Service that took care of all grocery needs. The product leverages the power of the milk railroad to deliver eggs, bread, fruits, veggies, and other groceries to the user’s doorstep. Order by 11pm, Get it by 7am. Supr Daily delivers your daily needs to your doorstep hassle-free.

Employed women and home makers want to provide fresh wholesome meals to the entire family. But, with a fridge full of stale groceries and a no-show from the milkman, it gets extremely difficult. This affects lakhs of Indian households increasing the need to hoard perishables which also increases wastage of food in the system overall. The product aims at solving this issue.

Design
Challenges

01

The product categorisation was extremely important to ensure ease of use as Suprdaily provided 5000+ products on their platform.

02

The second pain point is that the users are used to receiving deliveries in person.

03

For new users, the application needed to set the expectation with the user that his/her delivery will be dropped outside the main door in a bag and the user will not be interacting with the delivery executive.

04

To ensure the location entered by users were 100% accurate as there was no interaction between the delivery person and the user.

User
Research

We interviewed users who were managing groceries for their family and were comfortable with mobile applications.

Insights from
the User Research

  • A 25-35 y/o married woman in India, buying groceries for her family struggles to keep groceries fresh and cannot buy too regularly due to overheads.
  • People plan the meals they are going to cook at most a day in advance.
  • People have a list of staples which they place an order for frequently.
  • People frequently watch cooking shows & recipe videos on YouTube to provide something special for their families.
  • People are very aware of the price different platforms offer and switch platforms based on the price as well as discounts.

Wireframing

We, along with the stakeholders, spent around 7 weeks exploring solution spaces and gradually narrowing down on the hierarchy of infomation on the primary screens at the same time adding fidelity to each screen one week at a time.

Onboarding users to the concept of micro delivery

Precise delivery address

The delivery address was one piece in the workflow where the user’s input was crucial. In order to reduce the number of wrong addresses and locations, the user is asked to upload a picture of his/her house door. This helps the delivery executive narrow down on the right house since there are multiple houses on the same floor.

Importance to dairy products for existing users

We created a distinct product categorization for each grocery item for easy browsing. We wanted to provide users with an intuitive platform. But since dairy products are the USP of Supr Daily, we kept it as the first category for easy spotting.

We designed a unique, eye-catchy banner for each collection of products (for Eg : Healthy Breakfast) on the home page. This helps catch the user's attention and increases the number of clicks in this section.

The teal colour palette and the modern font family ‘Cabin’ was a refreshing change from other grocery applications giving Supr daily a unique identity and an easily recognizable Interface. We used a combination of line icons along with illustrations to ensure the experience is a visually engaging one. At the same time ensuring that the emphasis is always on the product, making the product the hero.

Illustrations with Bold Text

We used expressive illustrations along with bold text to set the right service expectation in the onboarding flow. This also helps reduce the drop offs during the login process.
